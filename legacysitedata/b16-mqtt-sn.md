## Introduction
The blog introduces MQTT-SN as "an optimized version of the IoT communications protocol, MQTT, designed specifically for efficient operation in large low-power IoT sensor networks." Many IoT projects fail due to unpredictable costs; data transmission is a major cost driver per device.

## MQTT
MQTT is a lightweight machine-to-machine messaging protocol with small code footprint and message size. It operates on publish-subscribe basis and includes built-in Quality of Service features.

## Benefits of MQTT-SN
- Shortened topic IDs (converting strings to two-byte aliases)
- A register function for alias registration
- A keep-alive procedure allowing devices to sleep when inactive

## The hidden cost of IoT at scale
Gartner predicted "around 20.4 billion IoT devices worldwide" by 2020, with spending reaching "$3.4 trillion" on endpoints and services. Hidden operational costs include data transmission, power consumption, and unreliable data issues at scale.

## Clear benefits vs. unclear costs
While IoT delivers clear benefits, costs at scale are often unclear. Edge processing, transmitting only necessary data, and selecting inherently efficient messaging protocols like MQTT-SN over alternatives like DDS or XMPP help control costs.

## Cost-effective scale with MQTT-SN
MQTT-SN's optimizations directly reduce transmission costs and power consumption.
