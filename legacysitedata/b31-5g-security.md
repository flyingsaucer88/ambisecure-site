## What is 5G?

5G is the fifth generation of wireless cellular networks, transmitting data over shorter distances than 4G LTE to improve speed and connection consistency. It supports more devices through new signal spectrums while using less power.

## How does 5G work?

Current 4G LTE networks face overload in major cities, necessitating faster infrastructure. The proliferation of internet-connected smart devices demands higher-capacity systems.

## Why 5G? / Possibilities

- Upgrading the IoT with battery-powered devices in remote areas
- Enabling smart cities and Industry 4.0 applications
- Supporting factory automation and infrastructure monitoring

## 5G Cybersecurity Concerns

- **Decentralized security** — "Pre-5G networks had fewer hardware traffic points-of-contact, which made it easier to do security checks and upkeep." The shift to software-based systems creates numerous routing points requiring monitoring.
- **More bandwidth will strain current security monitoring** — While current networks are limited in speed and capacity, this has actually helped providers monitor security in real-time.
- **IoT Device Vulnerabilities** — Many manufacturers lack cybersecurity prioritization.
- **Lack of encryption early in connection process** reveals device info that can be used for device-specific IoT targeted attacks.

### Cyber-Threats Listed
- Botnet attacks
- Distributed denial-of-service (DDoS)
- Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attacks
- Location tracking and call interception

## Consumer education on IoT cybersecurity is necessary

> "5G security creates an urgent need for the ecosystem — mobile operators, physical infrastructure providers, such as Nokia, Ericsson, and Huawei, vertical industries, such as automotive, and regulators — to work together."

Consumer education and potential FCC labeling standards for IoT devices are recommended solutions.
