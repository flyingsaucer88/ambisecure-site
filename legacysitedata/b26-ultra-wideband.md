## What Is Ultra-Wideband (UWB)?

UWB is "a short-range, wireless communication protocol" operating at high frequencies using radio waves across the 3.1 to 10.5 GHz spectrum.

## How does UWB work?

The technology functions by transmitting billions of pulses, with receivers translating these pulses into data through recognizing familiar pulse sequences. Pulses are sent "about one every two nanoseconds" for real-time accuracy. Effective range is approximately 30 feet. Distance measurement occurs through Time-of-Flight calculations.

## What are the possible uses for UWB?

- Hands-free access control
- Indoor navigation
- Device-to-device communication
- Automotive uses

Examples: Volkswagen's UWB-enabled car keys; BMW's mobile car access demonstrations from November 2019.

UWB advantages:
- Low spectral density making it "difficult to detect due to low power density"
- Resistance to interference
- Security benefits over Bluetooth and Wi-Fi technologies
