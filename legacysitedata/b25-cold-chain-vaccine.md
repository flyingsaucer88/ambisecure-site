## Introduction

The blog addresses cold chain logistics' importance in vaccine storage, handling, stock management, temperature control, and logistics management systems.

Ambimat Electronics highlights how the first COVID-19 wave exposed supply chain vulnerabilities in PPE distribution, including transportation capacity constraints, customs complications, warehousing difficulties, and inventory transparency issues.

## Inbound logistics and distribution as major supply-chain pain points

> "A single shipment by refrigerated truck (or 'reefer') can be worth up to $50 million in the case of pharmaceuticals." Cold chain logistics represents a "$15 billion market in the biopharma sector alone."

## Vaccine distribution as the next logistics hurdle

Different COVID-19 vaccine candidates require varying storage temperatures, ranging from -80 °C to +2 to 8 °C, creating unique distribution challenges.

## Effective Vaccine Management (EVM) Components

### Vaccine arrival
Procedures ensuring shipments reach destinations without cold chain breaks.

### Temperature control
Maintenance within WHO-recommended ranges.

### Storage capacity
Adequate cold and dry storage capacity.

### Infrastructure
Functional infrastructure for storage and transport.

### Maintenance
Preventive and maintenance systems for equipment and vehicles.

### Stock management
Effective stock management procedures.

### Distribution
Proper vaccine transport using passive containers and temperature indicators.
