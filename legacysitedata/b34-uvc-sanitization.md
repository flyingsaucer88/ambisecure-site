## Our take on the topic

Effectiveness depends on two criteria: product quality and proper usage method. The author draws parallels to hand sanitizers (requiring 70% alcohol) and handwashing (requiring 20 seconds).

## Harmful effects of UV-C

UV-C technology is an established sterilization method used in hospitals, banks, and water filters. However, it carries serious health risks including skin cancer development, reduced immunity, and decreased vaccine effectiveness.

## Safe uses of UV-C

UV-C exposure should be limited to inanimate objects only — keys, glasses, clothing, files, currency — never food or skin.

> "The World Health Organization (WHO) has issued a strong warning against people using UV-C light to sterilize their hands or any other part of their skin."

## Use of automation for safety

- Self-monitoring isolation chambers with app notifications
- Automated timers and action-based triggers
- Automatic kill-switches for accidental movement detection
- Controlled exposure timing based on material and UV source power

The conclusion advises consulting experts and selecting certified products before implementing UV-C solutions for business reopenings.
