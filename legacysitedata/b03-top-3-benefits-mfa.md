## Stronger Authentication

Reducing risk is every organization's goal. This is why the use of multi-factor authentication is growing rapidly. In a world where cyber attackers are constantly targeting user credentials and "over 80% of breaches are related to weak or stolen passwords," this kind of protection shield is required for stronger authentication.

The requirement of two or more factors for granting access reduces the risk of compromised passwords. It works as an additional layer of protection, reducing all different kinds of threats that could cost organizations millions.

Adverse effects caused by security breaches due to user passwords would have huge implications for both organizations and the user who trust it.

## Adaptive to Changes

With organizational changes and a remote work environment, the need for stronger and more advanced security solutions are needed, this is where MFA steps in to address more complex access solutions. This is referred to as Adaptive multi-factor authentication.

While MFA offers several layers of protection to users, Adaptive MFA assesses the risk a user exhibits whenever they try to access a system or information. For example, evaluating the user location and device.

A user logging in from the organization facility is said to be in a trusted zone, and may not be required to prove additional security factors. However, if the user logs in from a different location other than the office, using their personal device over an unsecured network, they may be required to verify themselves through second-factor authentication, as they are outside the trusted zone, using an untrusted connection.

Adaptive multi-factor has significant control over vital data. Users requesting to access such information may be required to verify themselves through two-factor authentication, or at the same time a third factor, before getting access to such critical information.

## Seamless User Experience without compromising security

Remembering passwords can be overwhelming for users with multiple accounts. This leads to the habit of creating weak, easy-to-remember passwords that can be compromised. What's more frustrating is the need to remember complex passwords, ultimately leading to password resets. A headache for the IT department.

Multi-factor authentication provides the organization with a secure environment. Users and their devices are protected without the cumbersome process of resetting passwords or following complicated security policies. Moreover, organizations can employ more than one form of authentication factors for users to choose from, or simply use additional authentication factors as and when necessary. MFA deployment is simple and easy to manage. It can be integrated with a large number of applications, thus, saving time and reducing the burden of dealing with password resets for the IT team.
