## NB-IoT and LTE-M

The blog discusses two Low Power Wide Area (LPWA) technologies for IoT applications. Both protocols enable low-bandwidth cellular communications for devices transmitting small data amounts while reducing hardware and subscription costs.

### NB-IoT (Narrowband-IoT)
- Europe-focused technology
- Module costs around $10, expected to decrease to $5–7
- Uses 180 KHz bandwidth
- Supports 100,000+ connections per cell
- Excellent indoor and underground penetration

### LTE-M (Long Term Evolution for Machines)
- Optimized for higher bandwidth and mobile connections
- Supports up to 1 Mbps uplink/downlink speed
- Includes VoLTE (voice support)
- Designed for mobile devices
- North America rollout focus

## NBIoT vs LTE-M

### Latency / Speed
LTE-M suits mission-critical applications requiring real-time communication (voice, emergency data, precision tracking). NB-IoT works better for simpler static sensor applications with acceptable delays.

## Use Case comparison

- Patient monitoring → LTE-M
- Fuel tank monitoring → LTE-M preferred
- Pipeline monitoring → either acceptable
- Smart waste management → either suitable

## Conclusion / Regional Availability

NB-IoT networks are developing in India through major carriers. LTE-M lacks significant adoption in the Indian market.
