## Introduction

> "LED lighting and communication networks are transforming street lighting."

Cities are upgrading to LED systems that reduce energy consumption by up to 50%. New York City expects $6 million annually in energy savings from replacing 250,000 street lights with LEDs, plus $8 million in maintenance savings.

Municipalities are installing lighting controls networks alongside LED upgrades. "Connecting street lights together in a computer-controlled network opens the door to a wide range of innovative capabilities."

## Industry and Market analysis

Northeast Group projects "19.9 million LED streetlights will be deployed in 2019" globally, with 3.1 million networked or "smart" streetlights.

> "Street lighting currently accounts for up to 40% of many cities' energy costs. This can be reduced by as much as 70% through LEDs and smart streetlights."

Cities can generate revenue through attachment rights, selling space to telecom operators for 4G and 5G equipment.

LED prices have stabilized, making the business case clear. Recent large-scale projects span the US, Europe, Latin America, and Singapore.

> "LED streetlights are now the default in much of the world, with even India reaching over 30% penetration."

The market includes hundreds of vendors, with some legacy leaders like Echelon and Harvard Engineering exiting.

## Conclusion

> "The over 303 million streetlights globally help form the backbone of the world's cities, towns, and highways." Previously viewed as a cost, "streetlights are now viewed as a revenue opportunity."

Standards organizations are becoming increasingly important as the vendor landscape evolves rapidly.

> "These benefits are now increasingly becoming understood, which will lead LED and smart streetlights to reach a projected 85% and 24% of the total streetlight market, respectively, by 2028. This will total a $50.4 billion market opportunity over the next decade."
