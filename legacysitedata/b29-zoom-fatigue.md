## Introduction

The article introduces "Zoom fatigue," defined as mental exhaustion from online video conferencing. It explains that unedited voices transmitted through the internet feel crude to listeners, making video meetings more draining than podcasts despite less participation required.

The piece references Harvard Business Review's research-based recommendations and notes that "multitasking can cost you as much as 40 percent of your productive time." Stanford researchers found multitaskers have worse memory retention than focused individuals.

## Recommendations

- **Avoid multitasking** — focus on the meeting
- **Build-in breaks** — schedule short pauses
- **Reduce onscreen stimuli** — minimize video windows during calls; use plain backgrounds
- **Make virtual social events opt-in** — make them optional
- **Switch to phone calls or emails** — don't default to video
- **For external calls, avoid defaulting to video** — especially with people you don't know well
