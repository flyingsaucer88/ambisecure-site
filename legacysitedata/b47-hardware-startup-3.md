After validating an idea and finding interested customers, entrepreneurs must demonstrate something tangible: a Proof of Concept (POC).

## Three key purposes of creating a POC

1. Validate that the proposed idea can actually be delivered as promised
2. Convert abstract concepts into concrete demonstrations that build credibility
3. Determine a preliminary feature set, clarifying what's feasible at the POC stage versus what belongs on the product roadmap

## Critical manufacturing cost consideration

Understanding manufacturing costs verifies market viability. If costs exceed what customers will pay, entrepreneurs should consider three approaches:
- Modify features to reduce expenses
- Pivot toward audiences with higher budgets
- Leverage economies of scale

## Conclusion

The POC process informs the development of a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) and product roadmap. Treat the POC as a strategic exercise rather than a formality, as these decisions significantly impact the company's future success.
