## What is LPWAN?

WAN stands for Wide Area Network — networks covering large geographic areas like cities or countries (cellular networks, internet, landlines). LPWAN (Low Power Wide Area Network) extends WAN functionality through wireless communications linking devices over extended ranges using minimal power.

LPWANs operate at slower speeds compared to Wi-Fi or 4G — managing around 125 KB/s per channel at best, with some operating at 12 B/s. This is acceptable because "the size of the payload is really low and the focus is more on the longer battery life." The technology suits battery-operated sensors requiring years of operation between replacements.

## What are the types of LPWANs?

**Cellular-based:**
- NB-IoT
- LTE-M / CAT-M1

**Non-cellular:**
- LoRa
- SigFox
- Weightless

## Cellular network based LPWANs

**LTE-M** is the most popular cellular LPWAN option, utilizing existing smartphone infrastructure and offering speeds up to 125 KB/s. Telecom providers can implement LTE-M gateways through software additions to cellular towers without hardware modifications. Major Indian cellular providers do not offer LTE-M networks today.

**NB-IoT** operates at approximately 30 KB/s — roughly one-quarter of LTE-M speeds. It employs DSSS modulation rather than standard LTE protocols and can function on LTE or GSM bands at 180 kHz. NB-IoT requires no gateway; devices transmit data directly to servers if infrastructure exists. Demands significantly less power than LTE-M and is reliable for high-priority monitoring applications.

## Non cellular network based LPWANs

**LoRa (LoRaWAN)** stands as the most widespread non-cellular LPWAN. Designed to "wirelessly connect battery operated 'things' to the internet in regional, national or global networks."

**SigFox** is engineered to support mass IoT deployments through extended battery life, reduced device costs, affordable connectivity, high network capacity, and extended range. Devices aren't attached to specific base stations; broadcasted messages reach any base station within range — typically three on average.

**Weightless**, created by Ubiik, provides wireless connectivity for IoT LPWAN applications, operating in both licensed and unlicensed sub-1 GHz spectrum. The Weightless SIG established it as an open standard. Adoption remains limited. Nwave smart parking services utilize a proprietary Weightless N variant with widespread deployment.
